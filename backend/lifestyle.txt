LIFESTYLE & RECOVERY GUIDE

SLEEP: 7-9 hours minimum, athletes need 8-10 hours. Same bedtime daily, dark cool room (18-20°C), no screens 1hr before bed, no caffeine after 2pm, no food 2-3hr before bed
SLEEP TIPS: Get sunlight within 30min of waking, no alcohol 3-4hr before bed, blackout curtains
SLEEP FOR ATHLETES: 8-10 hours, naps 20-30min between 1-3pm, sleep affects recovery and hormones, poor sleep = higher injury risk

REST DAY TIPS: Active recovery (walking, light stretching, yoga), foam rolling 10-15min, massage, one full rest day per week

RECOVERY SIGNALS (good): decreasing soreness, increasing strength, better energy
OVERTRAINING SIGNS: constant fatigue, weaker performance, poor sleep, joint pain

STRETCHING ROUTINE (hold 30 sec each): Quad stretch, hamstring stretch, chest stretch, glute stretch, child's pose 60 sec
DAILY MOBILITY (10 min): Cat-cow 10 reps, hip circles, leg swings, arm circles, ankle rotations
FOAM ROLLING: roll slowly 1 inch/sec, stop on tender spots 30 sec, never roll joints or lower back

INJURY PREVENTION: Warm up 10 min, dynamic stretches before lifting, static stretches after, use proper form, don't ego lift, deload every 6-8 weeks
WARM-UP: light cardio 5-10 min, mobility, activation sets with light weight

INJURY RECOVERY: Rest until pain free, maintain non-injured body parts, see physiotherapist, return slowly (reduce weight 50% first week)

CONSISTENCY TIPS: Show up even on bad days (just do 5 min), focus on behavior not results, track habit streaks, prepare night before

PLATEAUS: Change one variable at a time, add diet break (maintenance 1-2 weeks), change rep ranges, decrease stress, increase sleep, take deload week

MOTIVATION: Set process goals ("workout 4x week" not outcome goals), workout with partner, reward consistency not results, remember why you started

AVOID BURNOUT: 1 full rest day/week, have off-season 1-2 months easier training yearly, vary workouts, have non-fitness hobbies

RESULTS TIMELINE: 2-4 weeks strength increase, 4-8 weeks visible changes to you, 8-12 weeks others notice, 3-6 months body transformation, 1 year major change

BEGINNER ERRORS: changing workouts weekly, not tracking progress, too much cardio no strength, bad form, not enough protein, no recovery days, training to failure every set

FITNESS FOR BEGINNERS (first 12 weeks):
Weeks 1-4: learn form, full body 2-3x week, 8-10 exercises 2 sets each
Weeks 5-8: add weight, 3 sets per exercise
Weeks 9-12: choose split, track all workouts

FITNESS OVER 40: warm up longer 15-20 min, prioritize mobility, more recovery time, emphasize form, 8+ hours sleep non-negotiable, joint supplements

FITNESS FOR WOMEN: menstrual cycle affects performance, follicular phase (days 1-14) more energy, luteal phase (days 15-28) higher fatigue, don't fear heavy lifting, strength training improves bone density

WHAT TO TRACK: weight daily, body measurements weekly, progress photos monthly, strength numbers, how clothes fit, energy levels, sleep quality
SMART GOALS: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound

GOLDEN RULES: Train consistently, track progress, eat enough protein, sleep properly, focus on compound lifts, don't overcomplicate
REAL FITNESS TRUTH: beginners improve fastest first 3-6 months, muscle growth is slow but consistent, fat loss is mainly diet-driven

DAILY CHECKLIST: 7-9 hours sleep, 3-4 liters water, protein with every meal, 8-10k steps, supplements, 10 min mobility
WEEKLY CHECKLIST: 3-5 workouts, meal prep done, weight check, plan next week, 1 full rest day

FATIGUE TYPES: Neural (nervous system), Muscular (local muscle), Systemic (full body)
FATIGUE MANAGEMENT: Train hard enough to grow BUT recover fast enough to repeat
Progress = Stimulus - Fatigue

GOLDEN RULES: Train 3-5x per week, eat enough protein, sleep 7-9 hours, increase difficulty over time
